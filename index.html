BoxBoxBox

BoxBoxBox is a work-in-progress multiplayer party game that focuses on emergent-narrative. Each player manages its own racing team, while the drivers, all controlled by a simple AI, pruduce each time a new realistic, exciting and unpredictable race. This AI exploits concepts like Steering Behaviours, Velocity Obstacle Avoidance and Swarm Intelligence to take efficient trajectories, overtake and avoid collisions while still remaining fairly simple. Playing this game should fell like watching a real race, with the players rooting for their favourite teams and drivers.

Disboard

Disboard is a C++ framework useful for studying and developing AIs for turn based games. It is written with performance in mind and uses multithreading to further speed up the AIs. It includes a simple game engine that can be expanded to implement any type of sequential turn based game and several AIs that can play these games. This projects benefits from the HPC (High Performance Computing) facility of the University of Parma, Italy

Outro

I was inspired by the ending credits of Avengers: The Age of Ultron and decided to reproduce it. I extracted the models from the game 'League of Legends', and imported them into Blender. After a quick fix of some rigs, I started working on each pose, focusing on the interactions between characters and what story the sculpture would tell. Once that everything was in place, I started studying the camera movements, trying to visualize the story and the interactions I previously worked on. Finally, I exported the final renders from Blender and mixed them with the music in Premiere Pro.

Ctrl+Me Ctrl+You

Made in 48 hours during the GMTK 2020 Game Jam, 'Ctrl+Me Ctrl+You' is my first experience in a jam. The game placed in the top 30% of the over 5000 games submitted, and was really appreciated for its originality. The theme of the jam was 'Out of Control', so the game revolves around the concept of character control. 'Ctrl+Me Ctrl+You' is a puzzle game where you can control the body of your foes. But beware, they will controls yours!

Bolganone

Bolganone is a lightweight companion app for Fire Emblem Heroes, a popular gacha game for mobile platforms. Thanks to its features and completeness, the app quickly overcame other competitors and became the most popular companion app for FEH on Android, reaching 12k users. The app contains useful features like a database with informations about collectible elements, a unit builder and a floating overlay to check your units without having to close the game. Bolganone was also featured in different videos by PhonenixMaster1, a popular exponent of the community.

Sonar

A short game made as a training exercise for game jams. I gave myself a theme to follow and 48 hours to work on it. I chose the same theme used in the Global Game Jam of 2017: "waves". In Sonar, you play as a blind bat that relies on its sonar to perceive the environment around itself. The game revolves around the sonar mechanic, that allows the player to "see" the surrounding environment by standing still. However, every other element of the game forces the player to keep moving, thus generating an engaging and challenging gameplay.

LoR Wiki

LoR Wiki was the first companion app for Legends of Runeterra that landed on Android. The app features a database with all the cards currently in the game, a deck builder and a match simulator. It was selected in the first stage of the Legends of Runeterra Developer Challenge held by Riot Games. Unluckily, I had to retire my submission because the nation where I live (Italy) was excluded from the competition due to legal issues about the prize.
